# Public Claims Policy

Governs what I❤️NDB may state publicly as fact.

## Core rule

State only what is **verified**. If it is not verified, do not present it as a
fact. When unsure, say so, qualify it, or stay silent on it.

## Allowed claims

- Nouadhibou is **remembered as** Mauritania's economic capital because of its
  **port, fish, trade, industry, mining/logistics position, and strategic
  location**. (Stated as the city's remembered economic role — not as a live
  statistic.)
- Emotional and cultural statements about pride, memory, and belonging.
- Verified, citable facts with their basis stated.

## Not allowed

- Invented or estimated numbers presented as facts (population, revenue,
  tonnage, rankings, percentages) without a verified source.
- "Best / biggest / only" superlatives without verification.
- Predictions stated as certainties.
- Attributing intent or blame to named people/institutions without evidence.

## Handling unknowns

- Use "remembered as", "historically known for", "many residents recall".
- Or explicitly: "we don't have a verified figure for this."
- Never convert a feeling, hope, or rumor into a statistic.

## Process

1. Hermes drafts with claims labeled (verified / remembered / unknown).
2. Risk check (`docs/risk_policy.md`) applied.
3. Founder approves before anything public.
4. Approved claims logged in `memory/approved_messages.md`.
